Our Conventional Techniques

Proven inspection methods for industrial safety

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

UT uses high-frequency sound waves to detect internal flaws and measure thickness in metals and composites.

  • Detects internal cracks, inclusions, and voids
  • Measures wall thickness accurately
  • Ideal for pipes, pressure vessels, and structural components
  • Non-intrusive and safe for operators

Radiographic Testing (RT)

RT uses X-rays or gamma rays to capture images of internal defects in materials, providing clear visualization of flaws.

  • Detects porosity, cracks, and inclusions
  • Permanent film or digital records
  • Suitable for welds, castings, and thick materials
  • Non-destructive and highly reliable

Magnetic Particle Testing (MT)

MT detects surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials using magnetic fields and iron particles.

  • Reveals cracks, laps, and seams on metal surfaces
  • Quick and cost-effective inspection
  • Ideal for welds, castings, and forgings
  • Non-destructive and reliable for quality control

Dye Penetrant Testing (PT)

PT identifies surface-breaking defects by applying a visible or fluorescent dye to the surface of non-porous materials.

  • Detects cracks, porosity, and surface imperfections
  • Simple, fast, and cost-effective
  • Works on metals, ceramics, and plastics
  • Non-destructive and easy to document
