Our Advanced Techniques

Cutting-edge inspection methods for critical applications

Phased Array Ultrasonic Testing (PAUT)

PAUT uses multiple ultrasound elements and electronic beam forming to create detailed cross-sectional images of materials. This advanced technique provides:

  • High-speed inspection with comprehensive data collection
  • Ability to detect complex geometry defects
  • Permanent digital records for future comparison
  • Ideal for welds, corrosion mapping, and thick materials

Time of Flight Diffraction (TOFD)

TOFD is a highly accurate ultrasonic technique for detecting and sizing flaws. Key benefits include:

  • Precise sizing of defects
  • Insensitive to probe angle and material orientation
  • Excellent for weld inspection
  • Provides permanent digital records

Digital Radiography (DR)

DR uses digital detector arrays to capture X-ray images with exceptional clarity and speed:

  • Instant image acquisition with no chemical processing
  • Superior image quality and contrast sensitivity
  • Lower radiation exposure
  • Easy digital storage and sharing

Automated Ultrasonic Testing (AUT)

Automated ultrasonic testing uses computer-controlled scanners to inspect materials efficiently:

  • High-speed inspection of welds, plates, and components
  • Computer-controlled scanning for accuracy
  • Detects internal defects and thickness variations
  • Reduces manual inspection time and errors

Eddy Current Array (ECA)

Eddy Current Array uses multiple coils arranged in a specific pattern to generate detailed 2D and 3D images of surface and near-surface defects. Key benefits include:

  • High-speed scanning with real-time imaging
  • Detection of cracks, corrosion, and material loss
  • Ideal for welds, heat-affected zones, and complex geometries
  • Works on conductive materials including carbon steel and aluminum
  • No need for surface preparation or couplant

Infrared Thermography

Infrared thermography uses thermal imaging cameras to detect temperature variations that indicate potential issues. This non-contact technique provides:

  • Real-time thermal imaging for immediate analysis
  • Detection of electrical faults, insulation failures, and hot spots
  • Identification of moisture ingress and refractory degradation
  • Predictive maintenance for rotating equipment and bearings
  • Safe, remote inspection of energized and hard-to-reach components
